# Knuckles Game — Rules

A Farkle-style, push-your-luck dice game.

## Setup

- Six six-sided dice.
- Two or more players take turns in order.
- A match is played to a target score (default 2,000, configurable per game).

## Your turn

1. Roll the dice.
2. Set aside at least one scoring die.
3. Then either:
   - **Roll on** — re-roll the dice you did not set aside; your turn score keeps building.
   - **Bank** — add your turn score to your total and pass to the next player.

- **Bust:** if a roll shows no scoring dice, your turn ends immediately and everything built this turn is lost (your banked total is safe).
- **Hot dice:** if all six dice end up set aside, you get all six back and keep rolling on the same turn.

## Scoring (within a single throw)

| Combination | Points |
|---|---|
| Single **1** | 100 |
| Single **5** | 50 |
| Three **1**s | 1000 |
| Three **2**s | 200 |
| Three **3**s | 300 |
| Three **4**s | 400 |
| Three **5**s | 500 |
| Three **6**s | 600 |
| Straight 1-2-3-4-5 | 500 |
| Straight 2-3-4-5-6 | 750 |
| Straight 1-2-3-4-5-6 | 1500 |

**Four / five / six of a kind:** each die beyond the third doubles the three-of-a-kind value — e.g. four 2s = 400, five 2s = 800, six 2s = 1600; six 1s = 8000.

Singles other than 1 and 5 do not score on their own. Three pairs and two triplets do not score.

## Winning — complete the round

When a player's banked total reaches the target, the current round is played out so that every player has taken the same number of turns. Players who already acted this round do **not** get another turn. Once the round is complete, the highest total wins. A tie is settled by a sudden-death round among the tied leaders.

## Hero Points (Pathfinder 2e)

A player linked to a character may spend one Hero Point to re-roll any dice from their last roll — one die up to all six — which is especially useful to escape a bust. Spending decrements the character's Hero Points on their sheet.
